Championship Home Career

Championships with the Miami Heat

On July 8, 2010, LeBron James decided to leave the Cleveland Cavaliers to join the Miami Heat and unite with Dwyane Wade and Chris Bosh. In his first season with the Miami Heat, the trio had an outstanding career as everyone expected. Although they had a great run, they fell short losing to the Dallas Mavericks in the 2011 NBA Finals. The following year, LeBron seeked redemption and there was no way he was going to lose twice in a row in the finals. LeBron earned his first two championships winning back to back in the year of 2012 and 2013. LeBron James then again appeared in the 2014 Finals but lost to the San Antonio Spurs causing him to go back home to Cleveland.

Championship with the Cleveland Cavaliers

In the year of 2014, LeBron returned to the Cavaliers with a promise of bringing a championship back home to Cleveland. He made another appearance in the 2015 NBA Finals to make his 5th consecutive year. The year didn't quite end the way he expected losing to the Golden State Warriors. LeBron wanted to keep his promise so he then again made it to the 2016 Finals. Just like his first year with Miami, there was no way on Earth he was losing the finals twice to the Warriors. LeBron finally received his 3rd ring bringing the championship home back to Cleveland just like he said he would.
